What is Ethical Hacking

Ethical hacking is when people try to break into systems to find problems before bad hackers do. These people are called ethical hackers because they follow rules and help protect others. Their goal is to make computers and networks safer for everyone.

Why It Matters

Ethical hacking is important because it helps stop cyber attacks before they happen. Companies use it to protect personal information like passwords and bank data. Without it, hackers could easily take advantage of weak systems.

Common Tools

Ethical hackers use special tools to test systems and find weaknesses. These tools can scan networks, check passwords, and look for security problems. They are used only for learning and protection, not for causing harm.

Hacking Process

The ethical hacking process usually starts with gathering information about a system. Next, the hacker scans for weak spots and tests them carefully. Finally, they report the problems so they can be fixed and made stronger.

Rules and Ethics

Ethical hackers must always follow laws and get permission before testing systems. They should never use their skills to hurt people or steal data. Being responsible and honest is the most important part of ethical hacking.
